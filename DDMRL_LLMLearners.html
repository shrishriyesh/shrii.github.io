Verifiable Rewards

Enforcing Correct Reasoning in LLMs via Reinforcement Learning
CSCE 689 • Spring 2025

Project Overview

Goal: teach small language models to think rather than memorise, by rewarding both the final answer and the intermediate reasoning steps. A stronger "judge" LLM provides a verifiable reward signal for each trajectory, enabling reinforcement‑learning fine‑tuning (GRPO).

  • Solver (models): Gemma‑3‑4B‑IT variants.
  • Judge (model): DeepSeek‑R1‑Distill‑70B (API).
  • Datasets: GSM‑8K (grade‑school maths) & MATH‑12.5K.
  • Rewards: format ✓, answer accuracy ✓, reasoning correctness ✓✓.
  • Algorithm: Generalised REINFORCE Policy Optimisation (GRPO).

Training Pipeline

1️⃣ The base model generates a reasoning trace + answer. 2️⃣ The judge evaluates format, answer correctness & reasoning soundness, returning a scalar in {0, 0.5, 1}. 3️⃣ GRPO updates the solver in the direction of higher verified reward.

Baseline Setup

As a control, we implement the canonical Format + Accuracy reward used by DeepSeek Math / R1, omitting the reasoning check. Improvements over this baseline isolate the benefit of our Reasoning Correctness signal.

Experimental Setup

  • Base model initialised from Gemma‑3‑4B‑IT.
  • Judge queries to DeepSeek‑R1‑70B limited to samples whose answers are already correct (cost control).
  • Training budget: 1 k GRPO steps × 2 samples/step; run on a single A100 40 GB.

Discussion & Future Work

Verified‑reasoning rewards boost both accuracy and explanation quality with modest computational overhead. Ongoing work explores automated curriculum tuning, self‑refinement of the reward rubric, and extending the approach to code‑generation benchmarks.
